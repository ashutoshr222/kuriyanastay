Kuriyana Stay

about

If you’re listening to the name “Kuriyana Stay” for the first time you might wonder what does it mean. Kuriyana comes from Kured which means fog. Our location is surrounded by mountains, which results in fog passing through the location in regular intervals. More often than not & in the right season, fog accumulates so much that it gives an illusion of living in the clouds. Kuriyana Stay is unique because of its location. It is surrounded by reserved Ban tree forest of Rajaji National park, which makes it only property inside Binog bird sanctuary. It's a real treat for bird watchers. Kuriyana Stay also offers two beautiful trek routes for Bhadraj Temple & Jwala Devi Temple of Binog.

  • Commute to kuriyana stay :--
  • From Kitabghar mussoorie : - ( 8.4 Kms )
  • From Dehradun railway station :- ( 39 Kms )
  • From Jolly Grant airport :- ( 42 Kms )

The region is inhabited by Jaunpuri community and is primary agrarian, seasons and harvest plays an important part in their lives. Dance and music is very core to their culture. For the food served at Kuriyana Stay we have taken inspiration from regional cuisines. We as a small start-up is aiming to build sustainable businesses for locals and providing them with jobs near their home to stop their migration.

About owner:- Kuriyana Stay has been built on a piece of land which belongs to the Rawat family, and they belongs to Ghandiyala village. Some of the members have moved to Dehradun for education and work. Kuriyana Stay is managed by Sachin Rawat (youngest of the Rawat family).
